Predefined Disease Scenarios

Run multi-segment longitudinal simulations of common cardiorenal disease trajectories.
Each scenario progressively adjusts model parameters across sequential time segments.
Progressive Chronic Hypertension
Gradual increase in total peripheral resistance over 2 years, modeling untreated essential hypertension. TPR rises from normal to 1.6x while arterial compliance slowly declines.
TPR: 1.0 → 1.6× (linear ramp)
Compliance: 1.0 → 0.7×
HbA1c: 5.7% (constant)
Duration: 730 days · 24 segments of 30 days
Uncontrolled Type 2 Diabetes
Progressive glycemic deterioration over 3 years. HbA1c climbs from pre-diabetic to severely uncontrolled, driving nephron hyperfiltration, SGLT saturation, and downstream cardiac remodeling.
HbA1c: 6.0% → 10.5% (linear ramp)
TPR: 1.0 → 1.15× (mild rise)
Compliance: 1.0 (constant)
Duration: 1095 days · 36 segments of 30 days
Age-Related Arterial Stiffening
Isolated loss of arterial compliance over 5 years, simulating vascular aging without other comorbidities. Compliance falls from normal to severely stiff, increasing pulse pressure and afterload.
Compliance: 1.0 → 0.4× (linear ramp)
TPR: 1.0 (constant)
HbA1c: 5.7% (constant)
Duration: 1825 days · 60 segments of 30 days
Combined Cardiorenal Syndrome
All disease axes worsen simultaneously over 2 years: hypertension, diabetes, and arterial stiffening. Models the high-risk multimorbid patient progressing toward HFpEF.
TPR: 1.0 → 1.8×
Compliance: 1.0 → 0.5×
HbA1c: 5.7% → 9.5%
Duration: 730 days · 24 segments of 30 days

How it works

A differential evolution optimizer searches the disease parameter space (arterial compliance, peripheral resistance, HbA1c, nephron loss) to find the trajectory that drives the model's myocyte geometry through your specified targets. The simulated path appears as a dashed purple line on the chart.
